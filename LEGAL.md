# Legal Notice & Terms of Use

## 1. Purpose

BountyKit is an **authorized security research framework** designed exclusively for:

- **Authorized penetration testing** — engagements covered by a signed Statement of Work (SoW) and Rules of Engagement (RoE).
- **Bug bounty programs** — testing within the explicit scope defined by the program's published policy.
- **Educational research** — self-hosted labs, CTF environments, or systems you own.

You **must** have explicit written authorization before pointing BountyKit at any target. Unauthorized use is a criminal offense in most jurisdictions.

## 2. Jurisdictional Warnings

### Philippines — Republic Act No. 10175 (Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012)
Unauthorized access to computer systems, interception of data, and misuse of devices are criminal offenses punishable by imprisonment of up to 12 years and/or fines of up to ₱1,000,000. This tool provides **no exception** for security research unless conducted under a written agreement.

### United States — Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (18 U.S.C. § 1030)
Unauthorized access to protected computers carries penalties of up to 20 years imprisonment and fines of up to $250,000 (or twice the gain/loss). Courts have held that violating a website's ToS or scraping without permission may constitute CFRA violations.

### United Kingdom — Computer Misuse Act 1990
Unauthorized access to computer material (s.1), intent to commit further offenses (s.2), and unauthorized acts with intent to impair operation (s.3) carry penalties of up to 10 years imprisonment.

### European Union — Directive 2013/40/EU
All member states criminalize illegal access to information systems, illegal system interference, and illegal data interference.

### Australia — Criminal Code Act 1995 (Div 477-478)
Serious computer offenses carry penalties of up to 10 years imprisonment.

### Singapore — Computer Misuse Act (Cap. 50A)
Unauthorized access, modification, or obstruction of computer systems carries penalties of up to 20 years imprisonment and/or fines.

### Malaysia — Computer Crimes Act 1997
Unauthorized access to computer material carries fines up to RM 50,000 and/or imprisonment up to 5 years.

### Indonesia — UU ITE No. 11/2008, amended by No. 19/2016
Unauthorized access to electronic systems carries imprisonment of up to 12 years and/or fines of up to IDR 12 billion.

### India — Information Technology Act 2000
Unauthorized access, data theft, and system intrusion carry penalties of up to 10 years imprisonment and/or fines of up to ₹5,000,000.

### Canada — Criminal Code §342.1 & §430(1.1)
Unauthorized access to computer systems and mischief to data carry penalties of up to 10 years imprisonment.

### Brazil — Marco Civil da Internet (Lei 12.965/2014) & Código Penal
Unauthorized access to computer devices carries imprisonment of 3 months to 1 year plus fines.

## 5. Responsible Disclosure

If you discover a vulnerability using this tool during an authorized engagement, you must:

1. Document the finding with precise, reproducible steps.
2. Report it to the system owner within a reasonable timeframe (typically 24-72 hours for critical issues).
3. Allow the owner reasonable time to remediate before any public disclosure (typically 90 days).
4. **Never** exploit the finding beyond what is necessary to demonstrate and report the issue.
5. **Never** sell, trade, or publish unremediated vulnerabilities without the owner's consent.
